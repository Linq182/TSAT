TSAT: Radiation HeatX Coefficient - Reflective Concentric Cylinder Surfaces Library Block

This function computes the radiation heat transfer coefficient when the radiation term is written in a linear form (q = u*(Text - Tsurf)). This block is applicable for reflective concentric cylinder surfaces. The equation for calculating the heat transfer coefficient is given below.

$$ u = \frac{\sigma}{ \frac{1-\epsilon_{surf}}{\epsilon_{surf}} \frac{R_{ext}}{R_{surf}} + \frac{1}{\epsilon_{ext}} } \left( T_{ext}^2 + T_{surf}^2 \right) \left( T_{ext} + T_{surf} \right) $$

where

$$ u = radiation \: heat \: transfer \: coefficient $$ $$ \epsilon_{surf} = emmisivity \: of \: the \: surface \: of \: interest $$ $$ \epsilon_{ext} = emmisivity \: of \: the \: external \: surface $$ $$ \sigma = Boltzmann's \: constant $$ $$ T_{surf} = temperature \: of \: the \: surface $$ $$ T_{ext} = temperature \: of \: the \: external \: surface $$ $$ R_{surf} = radius \: of \: the \: surface $$ $$ R_{ext} = radius \: of \: the \: external \: surface $$

This block requires specific units be used. The units are clearly indicated on the input and output ports as well as the tables below.

This block accepts inputs of various dimensions as long as all inputs are consistent in dimensions.
