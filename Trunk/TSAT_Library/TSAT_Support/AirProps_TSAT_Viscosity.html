TSAT: Air Properies - Viscosity Library Block

This function provides an estimate of the viscosity of air given its temperature and density. The function uses empirical relations which are valid for dry air in the temperature range of 60K - 2000K (108R - 3600R) and pressures up to 2000MPa. The resource for this empirical correlations is:

Lemmon, E., and Jacobsen, R., “Viscosity and Thermal Conductivity Equations for Nitrogen, Oxygen, Argon, and Air,” International Journal of Thermophysics, Vol. 25, No. 1, January 2004.

This function is capable of evaluating the viscosity for various temperatures at the same time given an arrays of temperature and density.

Units are clearly stated on the inport and outport so assure that your inputs are in the specified units and the output is properly converted to your desired units after the calculation.
