TSAT: Simple Natural Convective Heat Transfer II Library Block

This function takes a simplistic apporach at modeling the natural convective heat transfer coefficient (h). The equation for calculating the heat transfer coefficient is based on empirical models and attempts to correlate the heat transfer coefficient with effective temperature difference between the surface(s) and or fluid relevant to the heat transfer. This block attempts to consider the temperature of the surface (Ts), the fluid (Tf), and an opposing surface (Tos)

$$ h = h_{coeff} \left| \frac{0.5 \left( \left( T_{s}-T_{f} \right) + \left( T_{s}-T_{os} \right) \right)}{\Delta T_{des}}\right|^{0.25} $$

This equation is a variation of Simple Natural Convection Heat Transfer and is based on the form of various natural convection models: $$ h = K Ra^{0.25} $$

As per the following reference:

Incropera, F.P., and Dewitt, D.P., “Fundamentals of Heat and Mass Transfer,” 7th Edition. John Wiley & Son. 2011.

K is a constant and Ra is the Raleighly number which is proportional to the temperature difference.
