TSAT: Non-Dimensional Parameters Library Block

Reference for the information in this help file: Incropera, F.P., and DeWitt, D.P., Fundamentals of Heat and Mass Transfer, John Wiley & Sons, New York, NY, 2002. Print.

This block computes various non-dimensional parameters that are related to heat transfer. Those parameters are defined below. A table will follow to define all of the variables used in the equations

Mach number (M): Ratio of flow velocity to the local speed of sound

$$ M = \frac {v}{a} $$

Reynolds number (Re): Ratio of inertial forces to viscous forces in a flow

$$ Re = \frac {\rho_f v L}{\mu} = \frac {\dot{m} L}{A \mu} $$

Prandtl number (Pr): Ratio of momentum diffusivity to thermal diffusivity within a fluid

$$ Pr = \frac {Cp_f*\mu}{k_f} $$

Grashof number (Gr): Ratio of buoyancy to viscous forces acting on a fluid

$$ Gr = \frac {g \beta \left( T_s - T_f \right) L^3 }{\nu^2} $$

Rayleigh number (Ra): Ratio of buoyancy to visous forces multiplied by the ratio of momentum and thermal diffusivities

$$ Ra = Gr Pr $$

Biot number (Bi): Ratio of heat transfer resistances inside of and at the surface of a body. It can be used to determine whether temperatures inside the solid body vary significantly.

$$ Bi = \frac {h L}{k_s} $$

Fourier number (Fo): Ratio of diffusive or conductive transport rate to the heat storage rate. Helps to characterize transient heat conduction.

$$ Fo = \frac {k_s t}{\rho_s Cp_s L^2} $$

Nusselt number (Nu): Ratio of convective to conductive heat transfer across a boundary

$$ Nu = \frac {h L}{k_f} $$

Stanton number (Re): Ratio of heat transfered into a fluid to the thermal capacity of the fluid

$$ St = \frac {h}{\rho_f v Cp_f} $$

Eckert number (Ec): Ratio of advective heat transport to heat dissipation potential

$$ Re = \frac {v^2}{Cp_f \left( T_s - T_f \right) } $$

Colburn j factor (jH): Non-dimensional heat transfer coefficient

$$ jH = St Pr^{2/3} $$

Peclet number (Re): Ratio of thermal energy convected by a fluid to thermal energy conducted by the fluid

$$ Pe = \frac {\rho_f v L Cp_f}{k_f} $$ Equation Variables:

This block accepts inputs of various dimensions as long as all inputs are consistent in dimensions. Units of all inputs and parameters should be consistent and total scale units should be used.
